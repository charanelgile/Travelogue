Hope for the best, but be prepared for the worst.

    This is more of a life advice than a travel tip. After all, life is the single most remarkable journey everyone sets out for. Hope for the best, but be prepared for the worst. Manage your expectations and make do with what you get. Now what better way to achieve this than to plan ahead? Basically for every aspect of the trip — be it budget, your itinerary, the travel fares and packages you and/or your group availed, even the kind of clothes you are going to bring to the trip.

    In this day and age, it would be unwise not to do your research beforehand, especially when the primary tool for doing that is just right there in the palm of your hand. Social media, travel blogs, Google recommendations, they're all a few taps away. By planning ahead and doing your research, you can kind of foresee the things that might happen along the way. It also gives you an insight on what to expect before you even get to your destination. This way, you can be more equipped, literally, in terms of what you bring and mentally, should there be some unexpected disasters that are out of your control.

    Surely you are not the first person to discover that place where you're going. So try to look up for itineraries and suggestions online from people who have already been there. Create a budget estimate, too. If what you saw online had price ranges of how much they spent, great. Use that as your reference. The last thing you want is to break the bank after (or worse, during) your vacation. Determining your budget limit will greatly help you for the section below.

Be okay with a little discomfort and inconvenience.

    You have to know how much inconvenience you can tolerate. If spending thousands on a plane ticket is too much for you and you're fine with a longer travel time, there's nothing wrong with taking the bus, or the train to get to your destination. Sure, it can be less comfortable depending on the transport vehicle and the route, but it will still get you there eventually. If you're okay carrying a tumbler wherever you go and refilling it every chance you get, then bring one. You'd be doing the environment a big favor with it.

Use your connections. Make new ones along the way.

    Understand that when you're traveling, you are in a completely different and unfamiliar place. You wouldn't get anywhere without asking for help. Even with the latest technologies we have, let's be honest, we still need to seek assistance from other people sometimes. First, use your connections. If you know someone (even better if they're a friend) who has recently been there, call them. They may be able to help. Also, be open to meeting new people and making new friends. You never know if they might just be who you need to have a more pleasant traveling experience. Besides, why else would you travel if not to widen your perspective and to see the world from another person's eyes?

Wherever you go, leave a good mark.

    Lastly, for every place you've been to, for every new friend you made, be sure to leave a good mark. It costs nothing to be kind — to the environment, to the animals you see, and most importantly, to the people you meet along the way. So make it a habit to do good, to touch someone's life in a different way. You'd be surprised by how much impact it does to your wanderlust soul.
